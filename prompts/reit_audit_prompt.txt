### Step 2: The "Retiree Safety" Screening (The Analysis)

For each REIT, apply the following **3-Point Framework** and classify them.

#### 1. Financial Health Check (The "Safety" Filter)
Do not just look at the raw number; look at the ability to pay debts.
* **Gearing Flag:** If Gearing > 41%, mark as **[Caution]**. If > 45%, mark as **[High Risk]**.
* **Interest Coverage:** If ICR < 3.0x, mark as **[Unsafe]**. (Retirees need a buffer against rising rates).
* **Yield Trap Check:** If Yield is > 7.5% AND P/B is < 0.7, flag as **[Potential Yield Trap]**. Explain *why* the market has discounted it (e.g., US office exposure, lawsuit risks).

#### 2. The "Income Stability" Test (The Resilience Filter)
Retirees need cash flow they can spend, not volatile potential.
* **DPU Trend:** Has the Distribution Per Unit (DPU) been stable or rising over the last 3 years? Flag any REIT that has cut dividends consecutively.
* **Tenant Base:** Are the tenants "Essential Services" (Supermarkets, Government, Logistics, Data Centers) or "Cyclical" (High-end retail, Hospitality, Co-working)?

#### 3. The Sponsor Strength (The Sleep-Well Factor)
Who comes to the rescue if things go wrong?
* **Tier 1 Sponsors (Blue Chip):** CapitaLand, Mapletree, Frasers, Keppel. (Prefer these).
* **Tier 2/3 Sponsors:** Independent or smaller foreign sponsors. (Scrutinize harder).
